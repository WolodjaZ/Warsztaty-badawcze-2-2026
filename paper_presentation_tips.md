# Paper Summary Requirements

- Papers can be found on [google scholar](https://scholar.google.com/), [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/). The paper should be related to the project topic you have chosen.
- You can use papers that are provided to the project description document, but paper can be presented only **once**.
- Papers (not counting the ones provided) should be maximum 4 years old.
- Paper **must** be peer-reviewed and published in a reputable conference or journal (e.g., NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, CVPR, etc.). It should have review process and should not be a preprint. You can check the review process on the conference website or on [openreview](https://openreview.net/). Like this [paper](https://openreview.net/forum?id=i5WnXNjwbR).
- You are need to send me potential papers you want to present week before the presentation for approval. I will check if the paper is suitable for the presentation and if it is not already taken by other students. You can send me the paper via Slack.
- Presentation should be around 20 minutes long, followed by a 5-minute discussion.
- Text on the slides may be in English or Polish, but the presentation should be in Polish.
- You can view the presentation we give in our weekly meetings at Profesor Biecek AI Center Seminar as an example of how to [present a paper](https://github.com/MI2DataLab/MI2DataLab_Seminarium).
- The presentation should be clear and concise, highlighting the main contributions of the paper, the methodology used, and the results obtained. Bellow I provide a points system for the presentation, but the most important is to show that you understand the paper and can explain it to others.

## For what you will get points

- Section-by-section summary (3 points)
- Author background research (who they are, from where, number of citations and main domain of research of each author) (1 point)
- Summary of the review process of the paper + what conference/journal it was published in (1 point)
- Clear contribution statement and what results prove the contribution (1 point)
- Understanding of the paper and ability to answer questions (1 point)
- Discussion with the audience (1 point)
- Overall quality of the presentation (2 points)
- Bonus point for analysis of papers that have cited the summary paper with focus on why they cited this paper (min. 3 papers, additional 1 points)
- Bonus point for what you think about the paper and how it can be improved (1 point)
